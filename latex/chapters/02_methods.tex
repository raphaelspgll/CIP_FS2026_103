\section{Methods}

\subsection{Data Source and Collection}

Historical OHLCV data for Bitcoin, XRP, and ICP were collected from \textbf{CoinMarketCap} using a
Playwright-based scraper (\texttt{src/coinmarket\_scrape.py}).
Playwright drives a headless Chromium browser to handle the JavaScript-rendered price tables.
The scrape covers daily records from 2022-12-08 to 2026-03-19, yielding \textbf{1\,198 rows per coin}
The seven fields are date, open, high, low, close, 24-hour volume, and market capitalisation.

\subsection{Preprocessing and Feature Engineering}

Raw files were inspected for missing values, duplicate dates, and OHLCV constraint violations.
All checks passed without issues.
No outliers were removed. Extreme price events such as exchange listings or market crashes are real
market signals that must be preserved.

Ten log-transformed features were computed per coin.
\textbf{Logarithmic scaling} was applied throughout because cryptocurrency prices are log-normally
distributed, log returns are symmetric and additive, and log-transforming volume and market
capitalisation removes scale dominance across the three assets.

A \textbf{stationarity analysis} showed that level features (\texttt{log\_close}, \texttt{ma\_7},
\texttt{ma\_30}, \texttt{log\_market\_cap}) are non-stationary and exhibit pairwise Pearson
correlations of $r \geq 0.91$.
Including them as predictors would produce spurious correlations with the target.
They were therefore excluded, leaving five stationary features for modelling (Table~\ref{tab:features}).

\begin{table}[H]
  \centering
  \caption{Feature set used for modelling (all values from day $t-1$).}
  \label{tab:features}
  \begin{tabular}{@{}L{4cm}L{9cm}@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Feature} & \textbf{Description} \\
    \midrule
    \texttt{log\_return}            & $\log(p_t / p_{t-1})$ — daily log return \\
    \texttt{log\_close\_open\_ratio}& $\log(\text{close} / \text{open})$ — intraday direction \\
    \texttt{log\_high\_low\_ratio}  & $\log(\text{high} / \text{low})$ — intraday range \\
    \texttt{volatility\_7}          & 7-day rolling standard deviation of log returns \\
    \texttt{log\_volume\_change}    & $\log(v_t / v_{t-1})$ — relative volume change \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

All features are lagged by one day before training so the model only observes information available
prior to the prediction date.
The binary target \texttt{price\_direction} is $1$ if $\log(p_t / p_{t-1}) > 0$, else $0$.

\subsection{Analysis Methods}

\textbf{Exploratory analysis} covered autocorrelation (ACF for lags 1 to 20), lag-1 Pearson
correlations between each feature and the target, cross-asset log return correlations, and rolling
volatility.

\textbf{Modelling} uses two classifiers evaluated under a strict chronological
\textbf{70\,/\,15\,/\,15 train/validation/test split} applied independently per coin.
Shuffling is not performed, as it would allow future data to enter the training set.
The models are Logistic Regression (\texttt{LogisticRegression(max\_iter=1000)}) and Decision Tree
(\texttt{DecisionTreeClassifier()}).
Model complexity was kept deliberately low to match the weak signal structure identified in the EDA.
Each model is evaluated on accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score, with accuracy as the primary
metric given the near-balanced class distribution.
