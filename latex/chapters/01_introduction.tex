\section{Introduction}

Cryptocurrency markets operate continuously and are characterised by high volatility, rapid price swings,
and strong sensitivity to market sentiment.
These properties make them an attractive but challenging domain for short-term predictive modelling.
Understanding whether tomorrow's price direction can be anticipated from today's observable market
indicators has direct relevance for trading strategies and risk management.

This project investigates whether publicly available daily market data for three major cryptocurrencies,
Bitcoin (BTC), Ripple (XRP), and Internet Computer (ICP), can be used to predict the direction of
next-day price movement.
The full pipeline covers data collection, preprocessing, exploratory data analysis, and binary
classification using machine learning.

Three research questions guide the work:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=0pt]
  \item Which market indicators have the strongest relationship with daily cryptocurrency price changes?
  \item How accurately can historical indicators from day $t-1$ predict price direction on day $t$?
  \item Which machine learning model achieves the highest classification accuracy?
\end{enumerate}
